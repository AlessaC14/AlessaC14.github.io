Frida Carbo

Chief Biscuit Officer · Senior Research Dog

Frida is a terrier-ish creature of indeterminate genealogy and maximum personality. She joined the lab in 2019 and has since contributed meaningfully to couch science, deadline management (by sitting on the laptop), and the development of treat-optimization heuristics. Her methodologies remain, at this time, proprietary.

Declared Interests

Research Programme

  • i.
    Bayesian Sniffing Sequential inference over scent-space: updating posterior beliefs about who has been in the park with each new olfactory observation. Evidence accumulation is real-time and continuous.
  • ii.
    Reinforcement Learning for Treat Optimization Multi-armed bandit formulation of the "look cute until snack appears" problem. Current policy achieves near-optimal reward rate. Exploration strategy: tilting head at 42°.
  • iii.
    Topological Data Analysis of the Sofa Persistent homology of optimal nap positions. Primary finding: the warmest location is always the one currently occupied by a human, suggesting adversarial dynamics.
  • iv.
    Causal Inference: Squirrel Attribution Does the sound of a squirrel cause barking, or merely correlate? Years of observational data suggest causality, but confounders (birds, leaves, wind) remain uncontrolled.
  • v.
    Walk Duration Forecasting Time-series model for predicting walk length from coat, leash, and weather signals. Current RMSE: 4.3 minutes. Primary failure mode: underestimating Sunday-morning long walks.
  • vi.
    Human Emotion Classification Zero-shot classification of human affective states from micro-expressions, gait, and tone of voice. Achieves 94% accuracy on "about to give a treat" vs. "not about to give a treat" — the only classes that matter.

Frida does not maintain a Substack. She has, however, been observed staring meaningfully at a blank notebook for extended periods, which the lab interprets as a sign of methodological rigor.
