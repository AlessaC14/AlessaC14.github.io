Applied Research

Alternative Proteins

The global food system is among the largest contributors to greenhouse gas emissions, land use, and biodiversity loss. Alternative proteins — plant-based, fermentation-derived, and cultivated — offer a path toward diets that are nutritionally adequate and ecologically tractable.

I work at the intersection of machine learning and protein science to accelerate discovery and reduce the trial-and-error burden in this space. That means Bayesian active learning for high-throughput screening, foundation models fine-tuned on non-model organisms, and multi-modal pipelines that integrate genomic, proteomic, and supply-chain data.

I also care about the policy and communication layer: making sure that good science reaches decision-makers and the public without being distorted by hype in either direction. I advise the Good Food Institute on computational biology strategy, and contribute to open benchmarks for the field.

Projects & Roles

Selected Work

  • ProteinFind: Open Benchmark for Alt-Protein Discovery

    An open benchmark suite pairing sequence databases from fungal, insect, and legume sources with standardized evaluations for foundation models. Designed to lower the entry barrier for ML researchers new to food science.

    GitHub Paper Dataset

  • Bayesian Active Screening for Mycoprotein Candidates

    In collaboration with a UK food-tech startup, applied Bayesian active learning to reduce wet-lab iteration cycles by ~40% in a screen for high-yield fungal biomass candidates.

    Paper (Cell Systems 2023)

  • Multi-Modal Farm-to-Fork Modeling

    Joint model over satellite imagery, supply-chain records, and fermentation sensor streams to predict protein conversion efficiency at farm scale. Published at ICLR 2025; code and data publicly available.

    Paper Code

  • GFI Scientific Advisory — Computational Biology

    Ongoing advisory role to the Good Food Institute's science team on ML/AI strategy for alternative protein R&D. Contributed to the 2024 State of the Science report's computational biology section.

    GFI Website

  • Protein Language Models for Non-Model Organisms

    Survey and fine-tuning study of ESM-2 and related architectures on insect and molluscan proteomes — organisms underrepresented in standard training corpora. Includes a benchmark and pre-trained checkpoints for public use.

    arXiv Model weights
